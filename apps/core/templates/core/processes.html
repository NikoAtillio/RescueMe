Understanding Adoption Processes

What to expect when adopting a rescue animal

The Adoption Journey

While each rescue center has its own specific process, most follow a similar pattern. Understanding these steps can help you navigate the adoption process more smoothly.

Remember that these processes aren't designed to make adoption difficult - they're in place to ensure the best match between animals and adopters, leading to successful, lifelong adoptions.

Typical Adoption Steps

1

Research and Preparation

Before applying, research different animals and breeds to understand their needs. Consider your lifestyle, living situation, and the time and resources you can commit.

2

Finding Your Match

Browse available animals on Rescue Me or visit local shelters. Take your time to find an animal whose personality and needs match your lifestyle.

3

Application Submission

Complete the rescue center's adoption application form. Be honest and thorough - this helps the center understand if you're a good match for the animal.

4

Initial Screening

The rescue will review your application and may contact you for additional information or clarification.

5

Meet and Greet

If your application passes initial screening, you'll be invited to meet the animal. This might involve multiple visits, especially for dogs, to ensure compatibility.

6

Home Check

Many rescues conduct a home visit to ensure your living environment is safe and suitable for the animal. They'll check for potential hazards and discuss any necessary modifications.

7

Approval and Adoption Fee

If approved, you'll pay an adoption fee that typically covers vaccinations, microchipping, and spaying/neutering. This fee helps support the rescue's ongoing work.

8

Bringing Your Pet Home

The rescue will arrange a day for you to collect your new pet. They'll provide information about the animal's history, health, and specific needs.

9

Follow-up Support

Most rescues offer ongoing support and will check in periodically to see how you and your new pet are adjusting.

Common Questions About the Process

Rescue centers want to ensure their animals go to homes where they'll thrive. The questions help them understand your lifestyle, experience, and expectations to make the best match possible. They're also responsible for the welfare of their animals and need to ensure they're going to safe, loving homes.

A home check is not about judging your housekeeping! The rescue representative will look for potential safety hazards, check that your garden is secure (for dogs), and ensure your home environment matches what you described in your application. They'll also discuss any specific requirements for the animal you're interested in adopting.

Adoption fees typically cover the cost of veterinary care the animal has received, including vaccinations, microchipping, and spaying/neutering. Some animals require more medical care than others, which can affect the fee. Additionally, the fees help support the rescue's ongoing work with other animals in need.

The timeline varies depending on the rescue center, the animal, and your specific circumstances. It can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks. While it may seem lengthy, remember that the goal is to ensure a successful, lifelong match.

If your application is declined, ask for feedback. It may be that the specific animal you applied for isn't suitable for your circumstances, but another might be. Rescues want their animals to find homes - they're not trying to make it impossible to adopt. Sometimes, they might suggest changes you could make to become eligible in the future.

Rescue-Specific Processes

Each rescue center has its own specific adoption process. When you find an animal you're interested in, check the rescue center's website or contact them directly for details about their particular requirements and procedures.

If you have questions about a specific rescue's process, we're here to help. Contact us and we'll do our best to provide guidance or put you in touch with the right people.
